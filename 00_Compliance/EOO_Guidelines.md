# Dos and Don'ts for Greek Dental Advertising

This guide outlines the regulatory and ethical standards for dental marketing in Greece, based on the **Code of Medical Ethics** and **Hellenic Dental Association (HDA)** guidelines.

## Dos
*   **Focus on Information**: Ensure all content is factual and based on scientific evidence.
*   **Maintain Professional Dignity**: Use visuals and language that uphold the integrity and honor of the dental profession.
*   **Be Balanced**: Present both the advantages and the potential risks/limitations of a proposed treatment.
*   **State Qualifications**: Clearly disclose if the dentist is a general practitioner or a registered specialist in a specific field.
*   **Include Practice Details**: Ensure the clinic's name, physical address, and contact information are clearly visible.
*   **Get Verified Consent**: Use "before and after" photos only if you have explicit, written consent from the patient.

## Don'ts
*   **Avoid Superlatives**: Do not use terms like "best," "number one," or "unrivaled" to describe services or equipment.
*   **No Comparative Ads**: Never compare your services to, or disparage, other dentists or clinics.
*   **Don't Promise Miracles**: Avoid creating unrealistic expectations or guaranteeing absolute success for treatments.
*   **Avoid Aggressive Sales**: "Canvassing" or aggressive solicitation for patients is strictly prohibited.
*   **No Misleading Pricing**: Avoid public declarations regarding pricing that give a false impression of an "extremely good offer."
*   **Don't Advertise Prescription Meds**: Direct advertising of medicinal products (like specific Botox brands) to the public is forbidden.
